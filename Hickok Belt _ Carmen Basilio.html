Meet the Hickok Belt Award® Winners

Past Hickok Belt Award ® winners are truly a select group – a veritable who’s who of sports icons. The award has celebrated the incredible careers of leading figures throughout sports. With names like Ali, Mantle, Brown, Namath, Palmer, Phelps and James it is an impressive list to be sure. To learn more about the amazing athletes behind the award including video highlights of their accomplishments, please enjoy the listings below.

 

Carmen Basilio (1957)

The son of an Italian immigrant onion farmer in Upstate New York, Basilio refined his boxing skills in the U.S. Marines and became a world champion in the welterweight and middleweight divisions. His everyman roots and relentless style made him a fan favorite. He reached the apex of his career when he upset world middleweight champion Sugar Ray Robinson in a fifteen-round decision on September 23, 1957, at Yankee Stadium, a victory that earned him his Hickok. After retiring from boxing with a 56-16-7 record, Basilio worked as physical education instructor at LeMoyne College and as a spokesman for Genesee Brewing Company. He was born on April 2, 1927, in Canastota, New York.
